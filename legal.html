Explore the laws and penalties designed to protect you from online harassment.

Your Legal Shield Against Online Harassment

Cyber harassment is illegal and punishable by law. Below are key regulations in place to protect individuals:

  • Privacy Protection Laws: Safeguarding personal information from unauthorized access or misuse.
  • Anti-Abuse and Threat Laws: Preventing the use of online platforms to spread abusive or threatening messages.
  • False Information Regulations: Combating the spread of false or misleading content with the intent to harm.
  • Child Protection Laws: Enhanced protections against predators targeting minors online.

Consequences for Cyber Harassment Offenders

Those found guilty of online harassment face serious consequences, including:

  • Fines: Monetary penalties ranging from €500 to €5,000, depending on the severity of the offense.
  • Prison Sentences: Offenders may face imprisonment for up to 3 years.
  • Restitution: Offenders may be required to financially compensate their victims for damages.
  • Harsher Penalties for Repeat Offenders: Individuals convicted of cyber harassment multiple times face more severe punishment.

The legal system is designed to provide protection, ensure accountability, and create a safer online environment for all.
